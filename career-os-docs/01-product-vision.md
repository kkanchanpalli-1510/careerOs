# Career OS — Product Vision

## The Core Insight

A career is not a document. It is a **graph of skills, decisions, experiences, and outcomes** with relationships between them. A resume is one lossy projection of that graph.

The product builds the graph once — and generates intelligence, projections, and guidance on demand, forever.

Every existing career tool is stateless. You arrive, paste a resume, receive an output, leave. Nothing learned. Nothing compounded. The next session starts from zero.

Career OS inverts this. The graph accumulates. A richer graph produces richer intelligence. That is the flywheel nobody has built on the individual side of the career market.

---

## The Main Value

The main value of the product is in the **intelligence of the graph** — what we can surface from the ecosystem of data, and the feedback we give individuals in their **forward-looking arc**.

The graph is not a feature. It is a knowledge asset that gets smarter over time.

---

## The Product Loop

### Stage 1 — Build the Graph
A structured onboarding session extracts the career graph through targeted questions — not "list your jobs" but "what decision did you make that nobody asked you to make?" Roles, skills, outcomes, decisions, and the edges between them render live as the user answers.

### Stage 2 — Project the Resume
Paste a job description. Claude traverses the graph — reasoning over which nodes and edges are most relevant to this specific role. The output is a tailored positioning statement, achievement bullets grounded in actual graph signals, and an honest gap analysis. The resume is the byproduct.

### Stage 3 — Set the Goal
Describe a career goal. Claude generates the target graph — the node types a person in that role typically has — and overlays it against the user's graph. Ghost nodes appear: the experiences they need but don't yet have. The gap is visual, specific, and actionable.

### Stage 4 — Close the Gaps
Every ghost node is clickable. Clicking opens a mini-interview: a targeted question to surface hidden experience for that gap. The answer enriches the graph — a new node appears. The growth path updates. The loop closes. The next session starts richer than the last.

## The First Wow Moment (90-Second Onboarding)

The onboarding must deliver something the user could not see through their own narrative. The graph sees the career as a structure, not a sequence.

**Flow:**
- **0:00** — Resume paste, one button: "Build My Graph"
- **0:10** — Graph builds live, nodes appear by type with staggered animation
- **0:45** — Strength insight types out character by character: opens with "You have..." or "You are one of the few people who..." — strength first, evidence second, identity reframe third
- **1:15** — Three non-obvious career directions revealed, grounded in graph topology
- **1:45** — "Which of these feels most alive?" seeds the goal graph
- **2:30** — Four targeted questions enrich the graph, each correcting a resume blind spot

**The four questions and what they correct:**

| Question | Blind spot corrected |
|---|---|
| "Tell me about something significant you built that nobody asked you to" | Initiative pattern — never on resumes |
| "What do colleagues bring to you that they don't bring to anyone else?" | Tacit expertise — feels obvious to the person |
| "Which parts of your work made you lose track of time — and which felt like a tax?" | Energy signal — determines reachable branches |
| "What's the hardest decision you've made — not technically, but consequentially?" | Decision architecture — judgment quality |

## Three Career Branches (from graph topology, not job boards)

Each branch is non-obvious — derived from which node combinations the person has that are rare:

- **Branch 1** — Most immediately reachable, validates what they've built
- **Branch 2** — Emerging from graph, feels energizing
- **Branch 3** — Genuinely non-obvious, the one they haven't considered

## The Tone Principle

> Like a brilliant friend who has studied your entire career, genuinely admires what they see, and can't wait to tell you something about yourself you've never quite put into words.

The product celebrates first. Gaps come later — and they land completely differently when someone already feels seen.

## Competitive Position

Every existing product starts from a document and produces a better document. None compound. None learn.

| Product | Model | Gap |
|---|---|---|
| Teal | Job search management + ATS resume builder | No graph, no compounding, no forward projection |
| Kickresume | AI resume builder, strong templates | Document-in, document-out |
| Careergraph.me | Career graph + LaTeX resume compiler | Uses graph to produce better PDF — no goal delta, no interview, no forward projection |
| CareerGraph.ai | Upload resume → one-time report | One-shot, no persistence |
| LinkedIn | Professional graph at network level | Population-level graph, not personal. Optimizes for their network, not your trajectory |
| **Career OS** | Personal knowledge graph that compounds | The only product that builds a structured model of the person and uses it to deliver non-obvious insight in the forward-looking arc |

## Build Phases

### Phase 1 — Personal Intelligence (Now)
- Graph build from structured onboarding
- Resume projection from graph traversal
- Goal delta with ghost node visualization
- Gap mini-interview and graph enrichment
- Growth path tied to real nodes
- Non-obvious insight: 3 job families, core strength reframe
- Beachhead: Senior tech and data professionals

### Phase 2 — Cohort Intelligence (6–18 months)
- Market signal integration
- Cohort benchmarking at user scale
- Peer/manager signal layer
- "Your graph looks like people who became X in Y years"
- Early recruiter-side capability query

### Phase 3 — Ecosystem Intelligence (18+ months)
- Recruiter discovery layer — capability matching not keyword search
- Outcome tracking — did the advice work?
- Longitudinal feedback loop
- Enterprise licensing — internal mobility
- API layer — career graph as infrastructure

---

## The Moat

1. **The graph itself** — cannot be replicated by uploading a PDF elsewhere. Switching cost grows with every session.
2. **Compounding intelligence** — gets more valuable the longer you use it.
3. **The recruiter flip** — individual graphs queryable by capability, not keyword. Retention mechanic dressed as distribution mechanic.
4. **Outcome loop** — closes the feedback loop. The only product that improves from its own recommendations.
